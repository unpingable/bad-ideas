# Lineage

`bad-ideas` descends in style — not subject matter — from [`rhizome`](https://github.com/unpingable/rhizome).

`rhizome` was filed as a static design memo / failure model for federated rooms. Its README opens with the explicit refusal to be a project: *"This place is not a place of honor. What is here is not a project. Nothing valued is implemented here."* That refusal is the precedent.

## What `bad-ideas` inherits from `rhizome`

- Repo-as-evidence-locker, not repo-as-incubator.
- Markdown, doctrine, and gap ledgers as the artifacts; no daemon, client, schema, or "tiny prototype."
- Explicit framing that the design memo is the deliverable, not a stepping stone toward implementation.

## What `bad-ideas` does differently

- **Negative-pressure focus** — recurrent temptations to refuse, rather than positive design for one specific problem.
- **Per-entry receipt structure** — each idea has attempt history, isotope to extract, and a falsification gate.
- **Public-as-commitment-device** — bad ideas filed publicly are bad ideas that cannot be quietly resurrected later.

---

> Rhizome proves the containment style. It does not authorize the contained idea.
